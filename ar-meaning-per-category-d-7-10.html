Meaning per category - D

These signs indicate which driving direction you must follow, or on which side you may or must drive past the sign.

The following signs apply to all drivers unless otherwise stated on a sign:

D-1

Roundabout, mandatory direction of travel. This sign can be found on a roundabout and indicates that you have to drive the roundabout counterclockwise.

D-2

It is mandatory to pass the sign on the right. The arrow can also indicate the other direction.

D-3

This sign can be passed on both sides. This sign is usually on a center guide (traffic island or obstacle between two lanes).

The following signs indicate a mandatory direction of travel, without indicating a one-way street. They can occur in all directions (left, right, straight and combinations thereof).

Finally, this category includes signs that are especially intended for cyclists (and therefore also snorfietsers), or moped riders (and therefore also drivers of speed-pedelecs). They are placed in places where these drivers must enter the road or leave the road and enter the cycle path or cycle/moped path.
