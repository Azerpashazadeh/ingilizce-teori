Scope of driving licence B (passenger car)

A passenger car driving licence permits you to drive a passenger car, and also to tow a trailer with that car. However, maximum weight limits do apply.

Empty trailer weight

Weight of the trailer as delivered by the manufacturer (so this may include any spare wheels and tools that accompany the trailer, but not all extra accessories).

Maximum authorised mass trailer

Maximum total weight of the trailer (including load). That is the weight of the trailer (unladen weight) plus the permissible load (payload). To determine whether you can legally tow a trailer, always check the maximum authorised mass (‘toegestane maximummassa’) rather than the unladen weight.

Trailers up to 750 kg

To tow a trailer with a maximum authorised mass of 750 kg or less with a passenger car, only a B driving licence is required, even if the maximum authorised mass of the car and trailer combined exceeds 3500 kg.

Trailers heavier than 750 kg

Trailers with a maximum authorised mass above 750 kg may in some cases be towed with a passenger car with only a B driving licence. In this case, the maximum authorised mass of the combination must not exceed 3500 kg.

Heavier vehicle combinations

If the maximum authorised mass of the combination does exceed 3500 kg and the trailer weighs more than 750 kg, a B driving licence is no longer sufficient. To drive this combination, you must have an E driving licence (as well as a B licence). This licence permits you to tow trailers with a maximum authorised mass up to 3500 kg with a passenger car.
