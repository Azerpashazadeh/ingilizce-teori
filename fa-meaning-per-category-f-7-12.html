Meaning per category - F

In this category you will find a number of prohibitions and directives. These have to do, for example, with overtaking, turning around, taking precedence over narrowing roads and the obligatory place on the road of certain categories of vehicles.

The following signs indicate whether or not you are allowed to overtake:

The next two signs are always placed together. For example, when the carriageway narrows or you approach an obstacle. One sign is placed on one side of the situation and the other on the other side.

F-7

No U-turn. This sign is usually on central reservations just before an intersection, to indicate that turning is not allowed at the intersection. Usually this is too dangerous or hindering on these sections.

F-8

End of all prohibitions previously indicated by traffic signs. After this sign, for example, temporary reductions in the speed limit no longer apply. You often see them beyond roadworks.

F-9

End of all prohibitions previously indicated on electronic signs. After this sign, any prohibitions indicated on ordinary signs still apply!

F-10

Stop. This sign can indicate by whom or why the sign is applied. These signs are used, for example, by the crossing patrol (lollipop man or lady).

The following signs indicate that it is mandatory for these specific vehicles to drive on the specified carriageway or lane, or that this carriageway or lane ends.

Passing lane

Carriageway or lane that slow traffic must make use of so that they can be overtaken by other traffic. You will encounter these mainly on busier roads that also see a lot of agricultural traffic.
