Vehicle requirements

Motor carriage

All engine-driven vehicles. These may not require rails for mobilisation (e.g. train, metro, or tram). This does not include bicycles with pedal assistance. Attention: Does include trolley buses and speed pedelecs!

Driver of a motor carriage

The person driving a motor carriage or under whose immediate supervision a motor carriage is being driven (e.g. a driving instructor).

Holder of a motor carriage or trailer

Someone who rents, borrows, or leases a vehicle for an extended period of time. This does not imply or refer to the owner.

A vehicle must meet certain technical requirements before it can be operated on public roads. If a manufacturer releases a new car model, it must first be typeapproved by the Road Traffic Service (RDW) before it can be sold in the Netherlands.

Modifying your car in any way could invalidate the type approval, rendering your car unfit for public roads. This means the car no longer meets the permanent technical requirements and must be re-approved.

Registration number

Unique identifier consisting of a combination of numbers and letters that is issued to a type-approved and registered vehicle subject to registration.

Number plate

Rectangular plate on which the registration number is visible. This plate must be attached to the vehicle registered with this registration number.

Registration certificate

Proof issued to the owner or holder of a vehicle. Contains vehicle information and registration number.

National vehicle register

National database in which all registration numbers and associated data are stored.

Unknown owner

If it is unclear who owns a vehicle that is on a public road, for example due to the lack of a clearly legible number plate, the vehicle may be towed and held until the owner comes forward. Before towing a vehicle, a wheel clamp may be placed for up to two days as a way of tracing the owner.
